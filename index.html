About Ariella

Damilola Oyedeji, who writes under the pen name Ariella, is a poet, essayist, and literary critic exploring the intersections of language, identity, trauma, and resistance.

Her work has appeared in prestigious literary journals including LLIDS, Lolwe, Orange Blossom Review, Brittle Paper, The Belfast Review, The Talon Review, and Prairie Home Magazine, among others. Through poetry and critical essays, she examines atypical bodies, feminist narratives, and the politics of language.

Damilola is currently pursuing her PhD at Texas, where she continues to push the boundaries of literary criticism and creative expression. Her scholarly work interrogates trauma narratives through feminist lenses, while her creative writing gives voice to the silenced and overlooked.
